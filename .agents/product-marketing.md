## Product Overview

Web design and operational systems for service businesses that need clearer workflows and less admin.

**What it does:**
Stephen Mantle builds websites, workflow systems, and practical automations for service businesses. Every service offered is already running live at Mantle Studios — clients buy a system that has been proved on the operator's own business before it is sold to theirs.

Independent design + automation studio. Sits on the same shelf as: boutique web studios, no-code automation consultants, fractional ops/RevOps consultants. Differentiated by being one operator, not an agency, with productised pricing.

Service business (done-for-you builds) with productised scope. Outputs are websites, automation systems, research agents, content pipelines, booking flows, and AI readiness diagnostics.

**Business model:**
Fixed price, fixed deliverables, written down. No hourly billing. No surprise change orders. Entry point (Mantle Studios website builds): EUR 400. Bigger automation builds priced per scope. 30 days post-launch support included.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:**
The business has outgrown the system around it. Work gets duplicated because ownership is unclear. Updates live across inboxes, chats, spreadsheets, and memory. Manual reporting delays decisions that should be obvious earlier. People build workarounds because the real process no longer fits the work.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic agencies sell sites that look fine and capture nothing.
- AI tools sold as features (Notion AI, ChatGPT, Zapier alone) require the customer to design the workflow themselves.
- Hourly consultants drift on scope. The customer ends up project-managing the project manager.
- DIY templates look entry-level and do not reflect the quality of the work behind them.

**What it costs them:**
Lost revenue (cold quotes, missed enquiries), staff stress, slow cashflow, client frustration, and — for clinics — compliance risk. Evenings absorbed by admin that should never have reached a human.

**Emotional tension:**
Strong work, weak front door. The operator knows the business is better than the system around it. The website looks entry-level. The inbox is a backlog. Friday evenings are spent on Monday's admin. The fear is paying for "AI" or "a redesign" and ending up with the same problem in a new wrapper.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Boutique web studios + no-code automation consultants — most ship one or the other (site OR automation). Few ship both with proof already live on the operator's own brand.

**Secondary:** Generic agencies, Fiverr/Upwork freelancers, Squarespace/Wix templates — falls short on operational thinking; the result looks like a website but does not change how the business runs.

**Indirect:** Status-quo "we'll get to it next quarter" + hiring a part-time admin + asking the founder's nephew to redo the site — falls short because the same friction returns within weeks, and the cost is hidden in the operator's evenings.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Every service is **already running live at Mantle Studios** before it is sold. Proof of concept is not a slide deck — it is the operator's own business.
- Operational thinking first, tools second. The work starts at "where is the business leaking time" rather than "what software should we buy."
- Fixed price, fixed deliverables, written down. No hourly billing. Working drafts every week, not a big reveal at the end.
- One operator (Stephen), not an agency. The person scoping the work is the person building it.
- Companion brand structure: Mantle Studios for productised website builds (fixed entry point), Stephen Mantle for the builder log + operational systems and diagnostics.

**How we do it differently:**
Brief → Scope → Build → Handover. Weekly working drafts. Site or system goes live with a clean handover doc and 30 days of post-launch support included.

**Why that's better:**
The customer can see the system working before they buy it (on mantle-studios.com or stephenmantle.com). They sign a scope they can hold the builder to. They get something they can actually run themselves after handover.

**Why customers choose us:**
- They want a system, not a tool.
- They want fixed scope, not hourly drift.
- They want proof, not promises.
- They want one operator, not an account manager between them and the build.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I can just use ChatGPT / Zapier myself." | You can. The question is whether the workflow design is the bottleneck, not the tool. The cost is your evenings designing it. |
| "We don't have a clear spec." | You do not need a full specification. If you can describe the friction, that is enough — the diagnostic + brief turns that into a scope. |
| "AI feels risky in our context (clinical, regulated)." | Default is draft-only + human approval. AI runs inside explicit boundaries. Clinical judgement, sensitive messaging, and final care decisions stay human. |
| "We tried an agency / freelancer and it didn't move the business." | Fair. The difference here is the work starts at the operational friction, not the tool — and the system is already proved live on the operator's own business. |
| "EUR 400 / fixed price feels too cheap or too expensive." | The EUR 400 entry is the Mantle Studios brand-ready website tier. Operational system builds are priced per scope and sit higher. |

**Anti-persona:**
- Marketing teams at funded startups looking for an AI content agency.
- "AI content creators and solopreneurs" buying tools for the sake of tools.
- Anyone whose problem is *more reach*, not *less friction*.
- Buyers shopping on hourly rates or willing to drift scope.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:**
Lost revenue from cold quotes. A website that looks like a 2018 template. Admin that spills into the evening. The realisation that the business is better than the system around it.

**Pull:**
Proof they can see live. Fixed price, written-down scope. A builder who shows weekly working drafts rather than a reveal at the end. The promise that the system is handed over working and documented, not abandoned.

**Habit:**
"We'll get to it next quarter." Asking a friend or a nephew to redo the site. Sticking with a freelancer because switching feels expensive. Pretending the inbox backlog is normal.

**Anxiety:**
Paying for "AI" and getting nothing usable. A redesign that looks pretty and converts the same. Lock-in to a tool or stack they cannot run themselves after the build.
